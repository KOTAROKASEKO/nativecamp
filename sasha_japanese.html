Mastering Giving & Receiving in Japanese

Understanding あげる, くれる, and もらう

The Core Concept: In-Group vs. Out-Group

In Japanese, the verb you use for "to give" depends on the relationship between the giver and the receiver. Think of it in terms of your "in-group" (私/watashi - me, my family) and an "out-group" (everyone else). The direction of the gift (inward or outward) determines the correct verb.

あげる (ageru)

To Give (Outward)

Use ageru when you (or your in-group) give something to someone else (out-group). The action moves away from the speaker.

私

Giver (You)

友

Receiver

Example Sentences:

私は友達にプレゼントをあげます。

Watashi wa tomodachi ni purezento o agemasu.

I give a present to my friend.

母は父にネクタイをあげました。

Haha wa chichi ni nekutai o agemashita.

My mother gave a tie to my father.

くれる (kureru)

To Give (Inward)

Use kureru when someone (out-group) gives something to you (or your in-group). The action moves towards the speaker.

友

Giver

私

Receiver (You)

Example Sentences:

友達が私にプレゼントをくれます。

Tomodachi ga watashi ni purezento o kuremasu.

My friend gives me a present.

先生が弟に本をくれました。

Sensei ga otouto ni hon o kuremashita.

The teacher gave my younger brother a book.

もらう (morau)

To Receive

Use morau when you (or your in-group) receive something from someone. This verb focuses on the act of receiving.

友

Giver

🎁
私

Receiver (You)

Example Sentences:

私は友達にプレゼントをもらいます。

Watashi wa tomodachi ni purezento o moraimasu.

I receive a present from my friend.

弟は先生に本をもらいました。

Otouto wa sensei ni hon o moraimashita.

My younger brother received a book from the teacher.

Check Your Understanding!

1. 田中さんは私に花を＿＿＿＿＿＿。

Tanaka-san wa watashi ni hana o ______.

くれます (kuremasu)

Explanation: Someone (Tanaka-san) gives something to you (私), so the action is inward. We use くれる.

2. 私は妹にお菓子を＿＿＿＿＿＿。

Watashi wa imouto ni okashi o ______.

あげます (agemasu)

Explanation: You (私) give something to someone else (妹), so the action is outward. We use あげる.

3. 私はお母さんに時計を＿＿＿＿＿＿。

Watashi wa okaasan ni tokei o ______.

もらいました (moraimashita)

Explanation: The sentence focuses on the act of receiving. "I received a watch from my mother." We use もらう.

4. 友達が私の犬におもちゃを＿＿＿＿＿。

Tomodachi ga watashi no inu ni omocha o ______.

くれました (kuremashita)

Explanation: A friend gives something to your dog. Your dog is part of your "in-group", so the action is inward. We use くれる.
