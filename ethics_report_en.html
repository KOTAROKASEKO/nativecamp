Ethics Inquiry Report

— Reading, Questioning, and Rethinking Morality through the Bible —

Purpose of This Assignment

The purpose of this assignment is not to make you believe in religion. It is also not to attack or deny religion.

Instead, you will explore ancient moral stories that humanity has relied on for centuries, and then question them using your own thinking.

Assignment Instructions

  1. Choose one story from the Bible (Old or New Testament).
  2. Identify what the story teaches about good, evil, or justice.
  3. Question that teaching by presenting counterarguments, doubts, or alternative perspectives.
  4. Finally, explain either:
    • Why this teaching may still be useful in modern society, or
    • Why it should be revised or reinterpreted today.

Hints for Counterarguments

  • What if a different choice had been made?
  • Would the same judgment apply in modern society?
  • Are there people who are not protected by this teaching?
  • How would law, science, or psychology evaluate this rule?

Evaluation Criteria

  • You are not evaluated on how much you believe the Bible
  • You are evaluated on how deeply you think
  • Uncertainty, disagreement, and unfinished conclusions are welcome
  • There is no single correct answer
Important:
To question something is not to destroy it.
Questioning is an act of trying to make ideas stronger.

Final Question

“Why should we not kill others?”
Can you answer this question without relying on God?
